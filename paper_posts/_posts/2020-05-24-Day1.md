# How to Read a Paper by S. Keshav

<!--more-->

## Summary: Tips on how to read a paper in 3 passes

1. First pass
    * Read title, abstract, introduction, sub-section headings and conclusions
    * Should be able to answer 5 Cs:
        * Category: type of paper
        * Context: relation to other papers
        * Correctness: assumptions valid?
        * Contributions: main contributions
        * Clarity
    * 5-10 minutes
2. Second pass
    * Read paper, ignore proofs (for non-mathematical I'd ignore stats details)
    * Mark relevant references
    * 1 hour
3. Third pass
    * _Virtually re-implement_ the paper
    * Challenge every assumption and think how you would present the idea
    * 4-5 hours for beginners, 1 for experts

Durations are obviously just rules of thumb, reading time will depend on length, complexity and how much of the topic you already know. As well as your current level of stamina.


[Keshav, S. (2007). How to read a paper. ACM SIGCOMM Computer Communication Review, 37(3), 83-84.](https://web.stanford.edu/class/ee384m/Handouts/HowtoReadPaper.pdf)
