# Computational psychiatry as a bridge from neuroscience to clinical applications by Quentin J M Huys et al.

## Why I chose it

More detailed review of computational psychiatry.

## Papers to check out

Paper 5, 98, 132 and specially 139 from references.

## Content summary

Talks about the 2 main computational takes in the area: data driven and theory driven (RL models).

A big problem of diagnostics by classification of symptoms is the presence of common symptoms between disorders, and even for some disorders the diagnostic reliability is problematic. 

ML with MRI data works pretty well to distinguish schizophrenia from healthy controls.

Theory driven approaches include synthetic, algorithmic and optimal models. 

The review very briefly mentions ideas of combining both approaches.

## My questions and future work

It's a pretty good and concise review, I didn't write much about it because I will just go directly to it if I need references in the topic. Check out paper 139, relates fmri and EEG features to decision parameters in RL.


[Huys, Q. J., Maia, T. V., & Frank, M. J. (2016). Computational psychiatry as a bridge from neuroscience to clinical applications. Nature neuroscience, 19(3), 404.](https://www.nature.com/articles/nn.4238)
